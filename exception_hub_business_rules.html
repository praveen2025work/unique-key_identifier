Business Document EH-RULES-002 · DRAFT

Exception Hub
access & notification rules

Who can log in, what they see, when they can act, and who gets notified — written to be read once, with a worked example at the end.

§ 01 — Login

The user must hold a TB BB role in finportal

Roles live in finportal. When a user opens Exception Hub, the app reads their roles from finportal.

IfNo TB BB role

The user is blocked at the landing screen. They cannot view exceptions and cannot act on anything.

IfOne or more TB BB roles

The user is navigated to the dashboard. The dashboard shows the data they're entitled to, based on the rules below.

§ 02 — What each role sees

Four categories, four rules

Front Office Ownervia NPL FO data
Sees only exceptions for master books where they appear as FO Owner, Delegate, or Supervisor. Book-level access.
Product Controlvia PC L4 data
Sees only exceptions on Business Area L4s where they appear as the PC owner. L4-level access.
Risk · Regulatory · IT central teamFO-initiated only
Sees every exception where Front Office has initiated the workflow. Exceptions that are still pre-workflow are invisible to these roles. The UI surfaces only the workflow steps relevant to their role.
Governance Forumread-only · everything
Sees all exceptions regardless of whether the workflow has been initiated. Read-only — there is no workflow step assigned to this role, so no actions are ever available.

Note on naming. The "IT central team" role is what finportal labels as the FO IT role. They are the same set of users.

§ 03 — When can they act

Seeing an exception is not the same as being able to act on it

A user can act on an exception only when all three are true:

Check 1They hold a TB BB role for that exception's category
Check 2The current workflow step is configured for their role
Check 3That step is the active step on this exception right now

Any one missing → action denied. Governance Forum users always fail Check 2 by design — they have no step assignment, so they can view but never act.

§ 04 — Notifications

The L4 Governance table drives who gets emailed

The Business Area L4 Governance table holds discipline owners, supervisors, and the governance forum for each L4. It is not used to filter what anyone sees — it is used only to decide who gets notified when an exception advances.

Example. An exception on L4 FX_OPTIONS_AMER advances to "Reg Reporting Review". The notification engine looks up the Reg Reporting owner and supervisor for that L4 in the governance table and emails them. They open Exception Hub, see the exception (because Risk/Reg/IT roles see all FO-initiated exceptions), and act on it (because the active step matches their role).
